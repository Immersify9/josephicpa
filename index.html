About Imran Josephi

Three Decades of Financial Leadership

With over 30 years of hands-on executive finance experience spanning nonprofits, public accounting firms, oil & gas, higher education, and government. Imran Josephi brings the depth of a Big 4 background and the agility of a trusted advisor.

A licensed CPA since 2009 and AICPA member since 2015, Imran has served as CFO, Director of Finance, and Audit Manager for organizations managing portfolios from $20M to $200M. Operating his independent practice, Josephi CPA, since 2014, he has provided ongoing fractional CFO services to HUD-funded nonprofits — turning organizations that ran consistent annual deficits into ones that now report consistent annual surpluses, through improved budgeting, disciplined financial reporting, and the implementation of modern accounting systems.

"I have seen firsthand what strong financial leadership does for a nonprofit: one of my long-term clients went from running deficits year after year to consistently reporting surpluses – simply by getting the right financial systems, reporting, and strategy in place. That kind of transformation is what I bring to every engagement."
